FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is Rumor Stance Classification?

Rumor stance classification involves categorizing user responses to rumors into distinct classes based
on their expressed attitudes. The primary categories are:

1] Support: The user agrees with or endorses the rumor.
2] Deny: The user refutes the rumor's veracity.
3] Query: The user seeks additional evidence or clarification regarding the rumor.
4] Comment: The user provides a remark that does not contribute to assessing the rumor's truthfulness.

Why is Rumor Stance Classification Important?

RSC plays a vital role in detecting and managing misinformation on social media platforms. By categorizing
stances, researchers and platforms can:

1] Judge public belief in rumors.
2] Identify potentially harmful misinformation.
3] Enhance the accuracy of automated systems designed to flag false information.

How does context influence rumor stance classification?

Context plays a significant role in RSC as it can affect how users express their opinions. Factors include:

1] Previous Interactions: The history of conversations can provide insights into user sentiment.
2] Cultural References: Different cultures may interpret phrases or sentiments differently,
impacting classification accuracy.
3] Temporal Factors: The timing of a post relative to an event can influence how users respond to a rumor.

How do you evaluate the performance of Rumor Stance Classification models?

The performance of RSC models is typically evaluated using metrics such as:

1] Accuracy: The overall correctness of the model's predictions.
2] Precision: The proportion of true positive predictions among all positive predictions.
3] Recall (Sensitivity): The proportion of true positive predictions among all actual positives.
4] F1 Score: The harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing a balance between the two.

What types of data are used for Rumor Stance Classification?

RSC primarily utilizes data from social media platforms, online forums, and news articles.
Common sources include:

1] Twitter: Tweets related to trending rumors.
2] Reddit: Threads discussing various topics and claims.
3] Facebook: Public posts and comments on rumors.
4] News Websites: Articles that may propagate or debunk rumors.
